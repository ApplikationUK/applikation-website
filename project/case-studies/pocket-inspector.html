02Challenge & approach

A 30-year-old
process. Replaced.

Replacing paper isn't a tech problem — it's a behaviour problem. Our job was to build something engineers would actually want to use.

// THE CHALLENGE

Paper, transcription, & days of latency.

Inspection reports were handwritten on multi-part carbon forms, photographed on personal phones, and faxed or transcribed back at the office days after the visit. Three problems compounded.

  • Data integrity — every transcription introduced errors, lost photos, or missed sections.
  • Latency — by the time head office saw issues, engineers had moved on to the next site.
  • Adoption risk — previous attempts at digital tools had failed because they were built for the office, not the field.
// OUR APPROACH

Offline-first. Field-tested. AI where it matters.

We embedded with engineers for the first three weeks — riding along on inspections, reviewing failed digital attempts, and rebuilding the workflow from the ground up.

  • Offline-first architecture — every screen works without signal; sync happens when it can.
  • One-handed UX — designed for gloves, rain, and ladders; tap targets sized for real conditions.
  • AI-assisted reporting — narrative generation from structured input, not the other way around.
03What we built

A toolkit that
works in the rain.

Six core surfaces — three for the engineer, three for the back office. All connected. All tested in the field before we shipped a line of production code.

// FEATURE 01

Mobile inspection app

Native iOS & Android, optimised for one-handed operation. Branching forms with photo capture, voice notes, and signature collection — all working offline.

// FEATURE 02

AI report generation

A custom-tuned model that turns structured field data into a polished, audit-ready narrative report — in under 10 seconds, with the engineer always in control of the final draft.

// FEATURE 03

Real-time dashboard

A live operations view for back-office teams — every inspection visible, flagged, and actionable the moment a sync completes from the field.

// FEATURE 04

Sync engine & conflict resolver

A bespoke offline sync layer with deterministic conflict resolution — engineers never lose work, head office never sees stale data.

// FEATURE 05

Form builder

A drag-and-drop tool that lets the operations team launch new inspection types in hours, not weeks — without involving developers.

// FEATURE 06

Compliance audit trail

Tamper-evident, timestamped, geo-located records of every inspection — meeting the strict regulatory requirements of critical infrastructure work.

05Process timeline

From kickoff
to live in 14 weeks.

No big-bang launch. We shipped a usable v1 to a pilot crew in week eight, then iterated weekly with real engineers in real conditions.

// W01–02
Discovery

Field ride-alongs & baseline mapping

Two weeks shadowing engineers across three regions. We measured every step of the existing paper process — time, errors, drop-offs — and built a baseline against which everything would be compared.

12 ride-alongs 3 regions 8 stakeholder interviews
// W03–05
Design

Prototype & field validation

Clickable prototypes tested with real engineers — in actual gloves, in actual rain. We threw away two design directions before locking the third.

Figma prototypes 14 user tests 3 design pivots
// W06–08
Pilot build

First app in the field

A 12-engineer pilot crew received the v1 app. Daily feedback loops, twice-weekly releases. The AI report module shipped behind a feature flag in week 8.

Pilot crew 2× weekly releases AI flagged-on
// W09–12
Scale

Region-by-region rollout to all 340 engineers. In parallel, we built the operations dashboard and trained the head-office team on the new workflow.
